Here are a couple of my projects that I am proud of:

Traffic Light Project

During senior year of high school, I used the Bosch dataset to create a Computer Vision project. The project was comprised of a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) that takes an image of a car's dashcam with the coordinates of traffic light(s) in the image as input, and outputs whether each light was red, green or yellow.

COSMOS Website

This was a website that I created back in the summer between my sophomore and junior year in high school, during my four week long summer camp. California State Summer School for Mathematics & Science (COSMOS) is a residential summer camp aimed at exploring STEM topics with college faculty, beyond what high school covers. I was in Cluster 6, which included 2 classes: Computer Networking and Robotics.

Distinguished Secretary

At Piedmont Hills High School, I participated in Key Club throughout all four years. At the end sophomore year, I took the jump to running for and attaining the position of secretary. For the entirety of junior year, I was hard at work making our school's Key Club a better club overall, culminating in my personal online portfolio for distinguished secretary. At California-Nevada-Hawaii District Convention (CNH DCON) 2019, I received the award, given to only roughly 10 secretaries out of the three states. I used Google Slides to build this.
